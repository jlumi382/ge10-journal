    Midweek Sevice

    October 23, 2024
    Silliman University Church
    2 pts

    What?

    On October 23, 2024, at 6:30 PM, I attended the Midweek Service at the Silliman University Church, an event organized by the Department of Science and Technology Scholars' Association in Silliman University. The objective of the service was to bring students together for a time of reflection and worship. My role in this event was as an usher, where I was tasked with welcoming attendees, guiding them to their seats, and ensuring that the service ran smoothly. Although I was nervous about participating in this role, especially since it was my first time and I’m not very active in religious activities, it ended up being a fulfilling experience. The other ushers and members of the church staff were very supportive, and their guidance helped me feel more at ease.

    So What?

    Through this experience, I enhanced my public interaction skills. Before this event, I was anxious about stepping out of my comfort zone, especially since I don’t usually participate in religious activities. My initial expectations were to simply observe and maybe help a bit, but It was quite easy so I was able to do more than what I expected. Even though I was nervous at first, I found that my nervousness faded as the service went on, and I started to feel more confident in my duties. One of the people who left a strong impression on me was a fellow DOST Scholar, who was calm and kind, providing helpful guidance whenever I felt unsure. I appreciated how their experience helped me manage my own nerves. The most satisfying part of the activity was realizing that I could step up and be a part of something larger, despite not being active in religious settings.

    Now What?

    This experience has broadened my worldview by showing me that there are many ways to contribute to a community, even if it’s outside of my usual interests. I learned that stepping outside my comfort zone can lead to personal growth and help me develop new skills. Moving forward, I can apply this learning by being more open to participating in these types of activities, even if they are unfamiliar or make me feel nervous. I also plan to stay involved with the activities that the Department of Science and Technology Scholars' Association will come up with, as I realize how valuable it is to be part of a supportive group. This activity taught me the importance of being open to new experiences. If I could do the project again, I would try to interact more with the attendees, as I found that engaging with people is an essential part of the usher role.

    How This Changed Me

    Because of this activity, I am more open to stepping out of my comfort zone to experience new things.
