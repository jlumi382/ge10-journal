    Youth Worship

    October 20, 2024
    Silliman University Church
    1 pt

    What?

    On October 20, 2024, I attended a church service at Silliman University Church, where Rev. Jonathan M. Cal delivered a sermon titled Love and Justice: Faith and Action. The central message was based on Amos 5:24, emphasizing that justice and righteousness should flow continuously. The sermon highlighted the connection between faith and social responsibility, encouraging me to reflect on the message while participating in the service.

    So What?

    The sermon challenged the traditional view of the church's role on whether it should it only focus on saving souls or also address societal issues. It emphasized that faith and action must go hand in hand. I was particularly moved by the idea that spiritual growth is evident in our actions beyond the church, and how treating others and seeking justice reflects our faith. This experience bridged spiritual and social growth, highlighting the importance of living righteously in everyday life.

    Now What?

    This experience reshaped my understanding of faith, showing that spiritual maturity involves how I treat others, not just worship. It motivated me to think about my role in promoting justice and fairness within my community. I recognize the need to align my beliefs with my actions. If I attend a similar event in the future, I’ll take notes and engage in discussions to deepen my understanding.

    How This Changed Me

    Because of this activity, I am more aware that my spiritual growth is directly tied to my actions and contributions to justice and compassion in the world around me.
