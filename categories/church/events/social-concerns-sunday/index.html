    Social Concerns Sunday

    October 20, 2024
    Silliman University Church
    1 pt

    What?

    On October 20, 2024, I attended the Social Concerns Sunday service at Silliman University Church. The theme focused on justice, based on scripture from Amos 5:24 and Luke 18:1-8. The sermon, titled Flowing Righteousness, highlighted the importance of justice in our lives. The service included inspiring music from the Covenant Choir and engaging messages from the ministers. It was a meaningful experience to watch the community come together around such an important theme.

    So What?

    This experience deepened my appreciation for justice and community in faith. I didn’t have expectations, but the sermon’s relatability and empowerment struck me, especially when the people's tendency to not take action when they witness someone being opressed. The preacher’s passion left a lasting impression and reinforced the idea of using faith to advocate for justice. I appreciated the welcoming atmosphere of the church, which made the experience even more significant. It prompted me to reflect on how I can promote fairness and kindness in my daily interactions.

    Now What?

    This service inspired me to think about how I can incorporate justice and righteousness into my life. It emphasized the importance of persistence, not just in prayer but in standing up for what is right. While I'm not heavily involved in religious activities, I realized that faith can lead to real change. I aim to be more mindful of fairness and compassion in my relationships and decisions. I see the value in attending such events for spiritual and practical lessons, and I’d like to engage more actively next time.

    How This Changed Me

    Because of this experience, I feel more motivated to pursue justice and to embrace persistence and faith in achieving my goals. The next time I see someone being treated in an unfair and unreasonable manner, I will definitely defend them.
