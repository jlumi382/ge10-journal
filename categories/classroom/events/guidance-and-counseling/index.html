    Guidance and Counseling Group Dynamics Activity

    August 19, 2024
    ATA Hall
    1 pt

    What?

    On August 19, 2024, I attended a Guidance and Counseling Group Dynamics activity from 10:00 AM to 12:00 PM as a participant at the ATA Hall, which aimed to form bonds with my fellow College of Computer Studies (CCS) freshmen.

    So What?

    As a participant and spectator, I went in with no clear expectations since it was my first time hearing about such an event, and we were required to attend. From the title, I initially thought we might be working on some sort of programming project as a group. However, the activity turned out to be something far more essential and meaningful. The event's focus on getting to know my fellow CCS freshmen was something I found particularly valuable, as I often struggle to initiate conversations with others. The games we played, like the one where we evolved from an egg to a supreme being by winning rock-paper-scissors, carried deeper meanings than they initially appeared. This game, for instance, taught us the importance of perseverance in striving to become the best version of ourselves. I also enjoyed the dragon game, where we had to catch the other team's tail. The strategy and teamwork involved in that game helped me form strong connections with my teammates, and it was incredibly satisfying to see our plans come to life.

    Now What?

    Through these activities, I realized how teamwork and strategizing skills which was something that I lacked are vital in any group activity. The experience made me reflect on my tendency to tackle challenges alone, and I now see that there are limits to what I can achieve by myself. This event emphasized the importance of trust and cooperation within a team, and it has encouraged me to be more cooperative in future group situations. I can definitely see the benefits of this activity, as it fosters bonds and friendships among CCS freshmen who may not have known each other before. If I could participate again, I would strive to connect with even more of my fellow CCS freshmen.

    How This Changed Me

    Because of this activity I am a better team player. I've also become less shy when talking to people.
