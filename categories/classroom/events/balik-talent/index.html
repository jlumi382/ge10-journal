    Balik Talent Lecture: Big Data and IoT: Why They Matter in Innovation

    August 20, 2024
    ATA Hall
    1 pt

    What?

    On August 20, 2024, I attended the Balik Talent Lecture: Big Data and IoT: Why They Matter in Innovation, held at ATA Hall from 8:30 AM to 11:30 AM. My role in the event was a spectator or participant, who listened to the alumni's lecture.

    So What?

    Before the lecture, I only new the meaning of the acronym of IoT, knowing it stood for the Internet of Things, but the concept remained unfamiliar to me, especially when paired with Big Data. However, as the speaker began providing examples, my understanding and appreciation for these topics grew. One example mentioned smart buildings in Singapore, where automated lights would turn on when sensors detected darkness and off when it was bright. Another example was the use of sensors in Formula 1 cars, which track the car's and driver's health, generating vast amounts of data which is essentially Big Data. This data can be used to control various components automatically and remotely through IoT. This aligned closely with my interest in machine learning.

    Now What?

    This lecture opened my eyes to the possibilities of IoT and Big Data, inspiring me to consider these concepts in future projects. With this new knowledge, I can now create abstract pseudocode or flowcharts to plan projects and learn the necessary programming languages to bring my ideas to life. I am grateful for this event, not only as an opportunity for alumni to give back to their alma mater but also as a valuable learning experience for students like me in the College of Computer Studies, who needs to know about the vast fields of the tech industry.

    How This Changed Me

    Because of this activity, I am now able to understand the basic concepts of Big Data and IoT. If I achieve success in the future, I hope to give back to my school in a similar way, contributing to the education and inspiration of the next generation of students.
