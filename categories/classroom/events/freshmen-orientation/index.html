    Freshmen Orientation

    September 18, 2024
    ATA Hall
    2 pts

    What?

    On September 18, 2024, I attended the Freshmen Orientation for the College of Computer Studies (CCS), which began at 8:30 AM. As a member of the working committee, I was responsible for managing the technical aspects of the event, such as setting up the projector, audio, and operating the presentation. The purpose of the orientation was to familiarize the CCS freshmen with the college’s grading system, awards, facilities, and other services.

    So What?

    I was somewhat nervous because I wasn’t sure how to set up the audio system, projector, and other equipment at the ATA Hall. Fortunately, I was assigned to work with two other members of the Technicals Committee, who also happened to be my friends. I watched JP set up the projector and microphone, and now I feel more confident about handling these tasks in the future. Additionally, I wasn’t very familiar with the grading system or the criteria for awards, but now that I’ve learned about them, I know what to expect. This event gave me a lot of new information that will be helpful throughout my journey here at Silliman University.

    Now What?

    This experience expanded my knowledge about our college department. Now, if I ever need to shift programs, get an absence excused, or visit the library, I know exactly where to go. As a freshman, the orientation was really helpful because it made me feel less lost within the school. I hope that over time, I will become even more familiar with the different areas of the department, which will make my time here more comfortable and efficient.

    How This Changed Me

    Because of this activity, I am now more confident in navigating both the academic and administrative aspects of my college life. I feel better prepared to face future challenges, whether it’s handling technical tasks during events or understanding the policies and services offered by the college. This orientation has been an essential step in making me feel more at home in the College of Computer Studies.
