    Silliman University Campus Tour

    September 18, 2024
    Silliman University
    1 pt

    What?

    On September 18, 2024, from 1:00 PM to 2:30 PM, I participated as a spectator in the Silliman University Campus Tour organized by the College of Computer Studies. The purpose of the event was to familiarize students with the different buildings and the history of the university. It was a great opportunity to explore and learn more about the campus that I have been studying in for the past 1-2 months.

    So What?

    Initially, I thought I knew most about Silliman, but I learned interesting facts about the campus. For example, the orange doors in the College of Business Administration are for emergencies, and some believe a ghost of Alice Fullerton appears every Founder's Day in Alice Fullerton Hall. These details deepen my connection to the place I study.

    Now What?

    This experience taught me more about Silliman and its history, adding to my knowledge of the university. I believe this will help me navigate the campus better in my day-to-day activities and prevent me from getting lost. The tour made me feel more engaged with the school and has strengthened my sense of attachment to it.

    How This Changed Me

    Because of this activity, I am more appreciative of the rich history and unique features of Silliman University. I feel more connected to the campus and look forward to learning even more about the place that I now consider a second home. The tour was not just an educational experience, but also a meaningful journey that deepened my connection to Silliman.
