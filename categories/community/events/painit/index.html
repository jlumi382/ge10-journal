    PaInit-Paghatag ug kaInit

    September 16, 2024
    Silliman University
    4 pts

    What?

    On September 16, 2024, I took part in an event called PaInit-Paghatag ug kaInit, organized by the DOST Scholar's Association at Silliman University. This event was held in response to the ongoing challenges caused by Typhoon Enteng, aiming to provide snacks and coffee to the university personnel who were tirelessly working on campus despite the difficult weather. The event started with a Google Meeting orientation at 9:00 AM, followed by the distribution of snacks at 2:30 PM. My role was a lead organizer, along with my fellow DOST scholars from the College of Computer Studies.

    So What?

    At first, I was nervous about fulfilling my responsibilities, especially since I am still a bit shy around my fellow DOST scholars from CCS. However, everyone was very friendly, and I gradually became more comfortable. The experience helped me improve my socializing skills, and seeing the smiles of the university personnel despite the bad weather made the effort even more rewarding. It was heartwarming to offer them some comfort in a difficult time. This was also my first experience as an organizer in a community event, and it taught me a lot about how to plan and execute such activities. Overall, this event helped me grow in my ability to share warmth and care with others.

    Now What?

    Participating in this event shifted my perspective on community work. I used to think that my efforts alone wouldn’t make a significant impact, but this experience showed me the power of teamwork. Working alongside others who share the same motivations allowed us to accomplish much more than we ever could alone. This realization encouraged me to continue organizing community events with my fellow DOST scholars, as I now understand how important it is to collaborate to achieve common goals. Additionally, it strengthened my connections with the other DOST scholars from CCS and deepened my appreciation for the value of community-driven initiatives.

    How This Changed Me

    Because of this activity, I have gained confidence in my ability to take part in and lead future community events. It made me more aware of the power of small acts of kindness, and I am now more determined to continue contributing to efforts that help others, especially in times of need. This event has motivated me to stay active in organizing and participating in activities that strengthen the bonds within our community and help those who need support.
