What?

On October 20, 2024, I attended the Blacklist Cup 2024 at the Daro RUSI Field. Although I didn't know much about frisbee beforehand, this event was a great opportunity for me to learn more about the game. Watching the teams play helped me understand the basic rules, such as how players pass the frisbee to each other, the importance of positioning, and the concept of scoring points by catching the frisbee in the opposing team’s end zone.

So What?

The event made me realize how physically demanding frisbee is. It’s not just about skill, but also endurance. The game is played on a large field, and players are constantly running, pivoting, and changing direction. Watching the players maintain such high energy throughout the game gave me a new appreciation for the physical stamina required to play. This experience also made me think about how sports can teach us discipline, teamwork, and resilience.

Now What?

From attending the Blacklist Cup 2024, I learned the importance of pushing through physical exhaustion, especially when faced with long stretches of intense activity. Even though frisbee was new to me, I was able to connect with the game by observing how players worked together to move the frisbee down the field. It motivated me to improve my own physical endurance and learn more about the sport. It also reminded me how trying something new can be both challenging and rewarding.

How This Changed Me

Because of this activity, I am more open to exploring new sports and activities. I now appreciate frisbee and how it can challenge both the body and the mind. This experience has also sparked an interest in trying frisbee myself, further expanding my appreciation for team sports and fitness.
